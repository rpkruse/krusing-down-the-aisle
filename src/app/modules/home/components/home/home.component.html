Our story

From day 1 God knew Ryan and Sarah would end up together, he just had to wait for them to figure it out too. As Freshmen in college, Sarah and Ryan unknowingly first met in their intro class for computer science the fall of 2014. Fast forward 2 years to fall of 2016, Ryan confides in his friend Matthew Weis that he is ready for a relationship but wants some help to meet the right girl. Matthew being the good friend he is, reached out to his girlfriend, Leah, to see if she knew anyone looking for a relationship, or interested in a date. To Ryan’s luck, Leah had moved in with a bubbly, blue-eyed Texan who was a perfect match. However, when mentioned to Sarah if she was interested in a date with a computer science guy named Ryan, she thought Leah was referring to a completely different Ryan from Computer Science classes. It’s safe to say Sarah was pleasantly surprised to be meeting up with Ryan Kruse!

Leah and Sarah went over to a friend’s house for dinner which included Ryan. All the friends knew what was going on, but Sarah and Ryan just sat quietly not really partaking in conversion because of nerves. As the night concluded, they both retreated to their friends to gush about one another. A few weeks went by before Ryan finally asked Sarah out on an official date (which include Chick-fil-A, might I add.) I’ll never forget how perfect Ryan wanted this night to go. He took her to grab CFA and eat it while watching the sunset, then to go stargazing in Manhattan (a perfect night for Sarah). All of these events place between September and October, at which point all their friends were anxiously awaiting a title.

Finally, in mid-November 2016 Ryan and Sarah decide to be official with titles. The 2 of them continue their love affair with Ryan being the first to say “I love you” come January 2017 and growing together as a couple.

Now for the fun part: The Engagement story. In between finishing school, getting a job, becoming an adult, both Ryan and Sarah grew more and more in love with one another to the point Ryan knew this was it. He would propose to Sarah (only after his Mom took him to the jewelry store to get her ring cleaned.) At the store, his mother jokingly said that he should look at engagement rings for Sarah. While looking he found the perfect ring and knew it was meant for Sarah. Bought in December of 2017 (on Sarah’s birthday), Ryan had to sit on this decision for almost a year, stirring every day about plans, making sure to not mess it up or clue Sarah in. In that time, Ryan was able to talk to Ron (Sarah's father) for her hand, to which he said yes and welcomed Ryan to the family.

When November 2018 rolled around, Sarah had her bags packed (they had been packed for weeks mind you) for the trip of her life to Disney World at Christmas! Ryan on the other hand was excited and terrified at the same time. They hopped on their plane to Disney for a week of fun! After running around the parks for a couple of days, the day finally arrived for Ryan to pop the question. With the tremendous help from Ashley and Jameson, Ryan was planning to pull of Sarah’s dream proposal. The only downfall would be Sarah herself.

What I mean by that is, after the nighttime light parade filled with Christmas costumes and world class Disney magic, Sarah (for better or worse) had no clue what was happening after and asked for a pretzel, then… Only after sending Ryan into more stress and anxiety to prolong with question and hope to not lose the ring, he was finally able to corner Sarah for a “picture” taken by Ashley (It was actually a video, if you haven’t seen it, please do!). During that, Ryan said everything correct he called her “Sarah Joy”, snuck the ring out...only for Sarah to say, “You’re not proposing now,” (you can actually see her saying this in the video) only to find out he was! Thank God Ashely was able to capture that magical moment between them for the rest of us to witness, and a moment to share with their future children!

You always hear stories of couples getting together through mutual friends left and right, but for Ryan and Sarah, their connection seemed more than a mutual one. Had certain people not coincidently had life events lead them to K-State, the same friend group, major and as roommates, we may not have this love story today, thus confirming my suspicion God knew what he was doing. This may be the end of their story leading them to the wedding, but we all know their story is just beginning!
